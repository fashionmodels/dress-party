Why Are Traditional Wedding Dresses White

Written by Frank Jun 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Wedding dresses are often seen as the symbol of a bride's purity and innocence, and it's no secret that the majority of wedding dresses are white. But have you ever wondered why this is the case? In this blog post, we'll explore the history and significance behind the tradition of wearing a white wedding dress.

Choosing the perfect wedding dress can be a daunting task, and the pressure to find the perfect gown can make the process even more stressful. While some brides may be content with any color or style, others may feel obligated to follow tradition and opt for a white wedding dress. But why are traditional wedding dresses white in the first place?

The tradition of wearing a white wedding dress can be traced back to the Victorian era. Prior to this time, brides would simply wear their best dress on their wedding day. However, in 1840, Queen Victoria wore a white wedding dress for her marriage to Prince Albert. This was a bold move, as white was not a popular choice for wedding dresses at the time. The trend caught on, and soon white became the go-to color for wedding dresses.

In summary, the tradition of wearing a white wedding dress began with Queen Victoria's bold fashion statement in 1840. Today, white is still the most popular choice for wedding dresses, although brides are free to choose any color or style they prefer.

Symbolism of White Wedding Dresses

While the trend of wearing a white wedding dress may have started with Queen Victoria, there is a deeper symbolism behind the color choice. White has long been associated with purity, innocence, and virginity. This is why brides historically wore veils, which were meant to symbolize the bride's modesty and purity.

Additionally, white is a symbol of new beginnings, which makes it the perfect color for a wedding dress. The bride is embarking on a new chapter in her life, and the white wedding dress symbolizes this fresh start.

Breaking with Tradition

While white wedding dresses will likely always be popular, many brides are now choosing to break with tradition and wear dresses in different colors. Some brides opt for pastels or bold hues, while others choose dresses with intricate patterns or designs.

Breaking with tradition can be a way for brides to express their individuality and make their wedding day truly their own. However, it's important to remember that there is no right or wrong way to choose a wedding dress. Whether you choose a traditional white gown or a colorful alternative, what matters most is that you feel beautiful and confident on your wedding day.

The Evolution of Wedding Dresses

Over the years, wedding dresses have evolved to reflect changing fashion trends and cultural attitudes. In the 1920s, for example, dresses became more streamlined and less ornate, reflecting the flapper style that was popular at the time. In the 1960s, dresses became more colorful and less formal, reflecting the shift towards a more casual and laid-back wedding style.

Today, wedding dresses come in all shapes, sizes, and colors, reflecting the diverse range of brides who wear them. Some brides opt for traditional gowns with long trains and intricate beading, while others choose more modern styles with clean lines and minimalist designs.

How to Choose the Perfect Wedding Dress

Choosing the perfect wedding dress can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be overwhelming. Here are a few tips to help you find the dress of your dreams:

  • Do your research: Look at different styles and colors to get an idea of what you like.
  • Consider your venue: Your wedding dress should be appropriate for your venue and the overall theme of your wedding.
  • Think about your body type: Choose a dress that flatters your figure and makes you feel confident.
  • Don't be afraid to break with tradition: If a white wedding dress isn't your style, don't feel obligated to wear one.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the history behind the tradition of wearing a white wedding dress?

A: The tradition began with Queen Victoria's decision to wear a white wedding dress for her marriage to Prince Albert in 1840.

Q: What does a white wedding dress symbolize?

A: White is traditionally associated with purity, innocence, and new beginnings.

Q: Can brides choose a wedding dress in a color other than white?

A: Absolutely! Brides can choose any color or style they prefer for their wedding dress.

Q: How can brides choose the perfect wedding dress?

A: Brides should do their research, consider their venue, think about their body type, and choose a dress that makes them feel confident and beautiful.

Conclusion of Why Are Traditional Wedding Dresses White

While the tradition of wearing a white wedding dress may have started with Queen Victoria, it has since evolved to reflect changing fashion trends and cultural attitudes. Today, brides have more options than ever when it comes to choosing their wedding dress. Whether you opt for a traditional white gown or a colorful alternative, what matters most is that you feel confident and beautiful on your wedding day.
