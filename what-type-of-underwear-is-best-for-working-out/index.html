What Type Of Underwear Is Best For Working Out

Written by Aprilia Jun 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of constantly adjusting your underwear during a workout? Do you experience chafing or discomfort while exercising? Choosing the right type of underwear for working out can make all the difference in your comfort and performance. In this post, we'll explore the best types of underwear for working out and how to choose the right pair for your needs.

When it comes to working out, the wrong type of underwear can be a real pain point. Whether it's uncomfortable chafing, wedgies, or constantly having to adjust your underwear, the wrong pair can really hinder your workout. That's why it's important to choose the right type of underwear for your exercise routine.

The best type of underwear for working out is moisture-wicking and breathable. This type of fabric helps to keep you cool and dry during a workout, reducing the risk of chafing and discomfort. Additionally, look for underwear with a snug fit that won't ride up or shift during exercise.

To summarize, when it comes to what type of underwear is best for working out, look for moisture-wicking and breathable fabric with a snug fit that won't shift during exercise.

Compression Shorts

Personally, I find that compression shorts are the best type of underwear for working out. They provide a snug fit that stays put during exercise and offers support to the muscles. Additionally, the moisture-wicking fabric keeps me cool and dry, even during intense workouts. I highly recommend trying out a pair of compression shorts for your next workout.

Performance Briefs

For those who prefer a traditional underwear style, performance briefs are a great option. They offer a snug fit and moisture-wicking fabric, but without the added support of compression shorts. They also tend to be more affordable than compression shorts.

Boxer Briefs

Boxer briefs can also be a good choice for working out, although they may not offer as much support as compression shorts or performance briefs. Look for boxer briefs made from moisture-wicking fabric and with a snug fit to reduce shifting during exercise.

Thongs

While thongs may be a popular style of underwear for everyday wear, they are not recommended for working out. The thin strip of fabric can easily shift or become uncomfortable during exercise, leading to chafing and discomfort.

Choosing the Right Size

When choosing the right type of underwear for working out, it's important to also choose the right size. Underwear that is too tight can cause discomfort and restrict movement, while underwear that is too loose can shift during exercise. Take your measurements and refer to the size chart for the brand you are purchasing to ensure the best fit.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear cotton underwear while working out?

A: While cotton underwear is breathable, it does not wick away moisture as effectively as performance fabrics. This means that you may experience discomfort and chafing during a workout. It's recommended to choose moisture-wicking and breathable fabrics for working out.

Q: Should I wear underwear with leggings?

A: It's personal preference, but for added support and to reduce the risk of visible panty lines, many people choose to wear underwear with leggings. Choose a pair of underwear that fits snugly and won't shift during exercise.

Q: How often should I replace my workout underwear?

A: It's recommended to replace your workout underwear every 6-12 months, depending on how frequently you wear them and the quality of the fabric. Over time, fabric can lose its moisture-wicking properties and become less effective during exercise.

Q: Can I wear my regular underwear for working out?

A: While you can wear your regular underwear for working out, it's not recommended. Regular underwear is not designed for the rigors of exercise and may lead to discomfort or chafing. It's best to choose underwear specifically designed for working out.

Conclusion of What Type of Underwear is Best for Working Out

Choosing the right type of underwear for working out can make a big difference in your comfort and performance. Look for moisture-wicking and breathable fabric with a snug fit that won't shift during exercise. Compression shorts, performance briefs, and boxer briefs are all great options to consider. Remember to choose the right size and replace your workout underwear every 6-12 months for optimal results. Happy exercising!
