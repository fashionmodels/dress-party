Best South Indian Snacks For Pregnant Ladies

Written by Robby Jun 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a pregnant lady who loves South Indian cuisine but worried about what to eat? Do you want to know which South Indian snacks are best for you and your baby during pregnancy? Look no further, as we have compiled a list of the best South Indian snacks for pregnant ladies that are not only delicious but also healthy.

Pregnancy is a time when you have to be extra cautious about what you eat. You need to ensure that your diet is balanced and provides all the necessary nutrients for your baby's growth and development. However, finding the right foods that are both healthy and tasty can be a challenge, especially if you are a South Indian food lover.

The target of this post is to provide you with a list of the best South Indian snacks that you can indulge in during pregnancy without any guilt. These snacks are not only tasty but also provide essential nutrients that are beneficial for you and your baby.

In this post, we will be discussing the best South Indian snacks for pregnant ladies that are healthy, easy to make, and delicious. We will also be providing you with some tips and tricks to make these snacks more nutritious and flavorful.

1. Banana Chips

One of the most popular South Indian snacks, banana chips, is a great option for pregnant ladies. These chips are made from raw bananas and are deep-fried until crispy. They are not only tasty but also rich in fiber, potassium, and vitamin C. You can also add some spices like turmeric, chili powder, and cumin to make them more flavorful.

During my pregnancy, I used to love snacking on banana chips. They were my go-to snack when I was feeling hungry between meals. I used to make them at home with minimal oil, and they turned out to be delicious.

2. Murukku

Murukku is a crispy South Indian snack that is perfect for pregnant ladies. It is made from rice flour and urad dal and is deep-fried until golden brown. Murukku is not only tasty but also rich in protein, fiber, and iron. You can also add some sesame seeds or cumin to make them more flavorful.

When I was pregnant, I used to make murukku at home and store them in an airtight container. They were my favorite snack to munch on while watching my favorite TV shows.

3. Masala Vada

Masala vada is a spicy South Indian snack that is perfect for pregnant ladies who crave something savory. It is made from chana dal, onions, and spices and is deep-fried until crispy. Masala vada is not only tasty but also rich in protein and fiber.

During my pregnancy, I used to love snacking on masala vada with some mint chutney. It was my favorite snack to have during the monsoon season.

4. Paniyaram

Paniyaram is a healthy South Indian snack that is perfect for pregnant ladies who want to indulge in something delicious and nutritious. It is made from idli batter and is cooked in a special pan until golden brown. Paniyaram is not only tasty but also rich in protein, fiber, and vitamins.

When I was pregnant, I used to love snacking on paniyaram with some coconut chutney. They were my go-to snack when I was feeling lazy and didn't want to cook anything.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I eat these South Indian snacks every day during pregnancy?

A: While these South Indian snacks are healthy and nutritious, it is recommended to consume them in moderation. Too much of anything can be harmful, so make sure to balance your diet and include a variety of foods.

Q: Can I make these snacks at home?

A: Yes, all these South Indian snacks can be easily made at home with minimal ingredients. Homemade snacks are always healthier as you can control the quality and quantity of ingredients used.

Q: Can I add more spices to these snacks?

A: Yes, you can add more spices to these snacks to make them more flavorful. However, make sure not to add too much spice as it can cause heartburn and other digestive issues during pregnancy.

Q: Can I have these snacks during any time of the day?

A: Yes, these South Indian snacks can be consumed during any time of the day. However, it is recommended to have them as a mid-morning or an evening snack.

Conclusion of Best South Indian Snacks for Pregnant Ladies

South Indian snacks are not only tasty but also healthy and nutritious. They are perfect for pregnant ladies who want to indulge in something delicious and beneficial for their baby's growth and development. By including these snacks in your daily diet in moderation, you can ensure that you and your baby get all the necessary nutrients. So, go ahead and try these best South Indian snacks for pregnant ladies and enjoy your pregnancy to the fullest.
