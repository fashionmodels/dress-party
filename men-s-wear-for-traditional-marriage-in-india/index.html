Men s Wear For Traditional Marriage In India A Guide To Looking Your Best

Written by Aprilia Jun 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Weddings in India are a grand affair, and dressing up for them is of utmost importance. Men's wear for traditional marriage in India is a topic that can be overwhelming and confusing, especially for those who are not familiar with the customs and traditions. From choosing the right fabric to selecting the perfect color, there are numerous factors to consider when it comes to men's wear for traditional marriage in India.

If you're a groom or a guest attending an Indian wedding, you want to make sure you're dressed to impress. The attire for Indian weddings is steeped in tradition and cultural significance, and it's essential to understand the dos and don'ts before making your selection.

Men's wear for traditional marriage in India includes a wide range of options, from the classic sherwani to the more contemporary Indo-western outfits. The aim is to look elegant and sophisticated while still adhering to the cultural norms.

Understanding the Traditional Attire

When it comes to men's wear for traditional marriage in India, the most commonly worn outfit is the sherwani. It's a long coat-like garment that is typically worn over a kurta and paired with churidar pants or dhoti. The sherwani is usually made of silk or brocade fabric and is adorned with intricate embroidery or embellishments.

Another popular option is the achkan, which is similar to the sherwani but has a shorter length and a more fitted silhouette. It's often paired with churidar pants and is perfect for those who want a more modern look.

Choosing the Right Fabric and Color

When it comes to men's wear for traditional marriage in India, the fabric and color play a crucial role in the overall look. Silk and brocade are the most popular fabric choices for traditional attire, but you can also opt for cotton or linen for a more casual look.

When it comes to color, it's important to choose shades that complement the occasion and the time of day. Bright and bold colors are perfect for daytime weddings, while darker, more subdued colors are ideal for evening events.

Accessorizing Your Outfit

To complete your look, accessorizing is key. A turban or a safa is a must for grooms, while guests can opt for a stylish stole or shawl. Jewelry is also essential, and you can choose from a range of options, including necklaces, bracelets, and rings.

Tips for a Perfect Fit

When it comes to men's wear for traditional marriage in India, getting the perfect fit is crucial. Make sure to get your measurements taken by a professional tailor to ensure a perfect fit. If you're opting for a ready-made outfit, make sure to try it on and make any necessary alterations beforehand.

Personal Experience

As someone who has attended numerous Indian weddings, I have found that the key to looking your best is to strike a balance between tradition and modernity. I prefer to opt for a sherwani or an achkan in classic shades of cream or gold, paired with churidar pants. I accessorize with a turban or a safa and some statement jewelry for a touch of elegance.

FAQs

Q: Can I wear a suit to an Indian wedding?

A: While it's not traditional, you can opt for a suit if you prefer a more Western look. Just make sure to choose a suit in a classic color like black or navy and accessorize with a turban or a safa.

Q: Can I wear jeans to an Indian wedding?

A: No, jeans are not appropriate for an Indian wedding. Stick to traditional attire like a sherwani or an achkan.

Q: What kind of jewelry should I wear?

A: You can opt for a range of jewelry options, including necklaces, bracelets, and rings. Just make sure to choose pieces that complement your outfit and the occasion.

Q: Can I wear sneakers with traditional attire?

A: No, sneakers are not appropriate for traditional attire. Stick to traditional footwear like jutti or mojari.

Conclusion of Men's Wear for Traditional Marriage in India

Men's wear for traditional marriage in India is all about striking the perfect balance between tradition and modernity. Choose the right fabric and color, accessorize with the right jewelry and footwear, and make sure to get the perfect fit. By following these tips, you're sure to look your best and make a lasting impression at any Indian wedding.
