The Thrilla in Manila

One of the most famous fights in boxing history, the Thrilla in Manila was the third and final bout between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. The fight took place on October 1, 1975, in Manila, the capital of the Philippines. It was a brutal, grueling fight that saw both fighters pushed to their limits.

Ali, who was known for his quickness and agility, was forced to rely on his toughness and resilience as Frazier relentlessly attacked him with powerful punches. In the end, Ali emerged victorious, but only after enduring 14 rounds of punishment from Frazier. The fight is widely regarded as one of the greatest in boxing history and is a must-watch for any boxing fan.

The Rumble in the Jungle

The Rumble in the Jungle was a historic fight between Muhammad Ali and George Foreman. The fight took place on October 30, 1974, in Kinshasa, Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of the Congo). At the time, Foreman was the reigning heavyweight champion and was widely regarded as unbeatable.

However, Ali devised a new strategy for the fight, which he dubbed the "rope-a-dope." Instead of trying to outmuscle Foreman, Ali allowed him to tire himself out by absorbing punches against the ropes. In the eighth round, Ali saw his opportunity and unleashed a flurry of punches that knocked Foreman out. The fight is considered one of the greatest upsets in boxing history and is a true classic.

The Fight of the Century

The Fight of the Century was the first bout between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. The fight took place on March 8, 1971, at Madison Square Garden in New York City. It was a highly anticipated fight, as both fighters were undefeated at the time.

The fight was a brutal, back-and-forth battle that saw both fighters take and deliver punishing blows. In the end, Frazier emerged victorious, handing Ali his first professional loss. The fight is considered one of the greatest in boxing history and is a must-watch for any fan of the sport.

The Battle of the Century

The Battle of the Century was a historic fight between Jack Johnson and James J. Jeffries. The fight took place on July 4, 1910, in Reno, Nevada. At the time, Johnson was the first black heavyweight champion, and Jeffries was coming out of retirement to try and take the title from him.

The fight was a major event, with tens of thousands of people coming to see it. In the end, Johnson emerged victorious, solidifying his place in history as one of the greatest fighters of all time. The fight is considered a landmark moment in boxing history and is a must-watch for anyone interested in the sport.
