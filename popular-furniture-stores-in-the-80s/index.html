Popular Furniture Stores In The 80S A Nostalgic Look Back

Written by Aprilia Jun 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you remember the days of neon lights, big hair, and shoulder pads? How about the furniture stores that were popular during that era? If you're feeling nostalgic, let's take a trip down memory lane and explore the popular furniture stores in the 80s.

Back in the 80s, furniture shopping was a bit different than it is today. You couldn't just browse online and have furniture delivered to your doorstep. Instead, you had to physically go to a store and spend hours browsing through different options. This was not only time-consuming, but it could also be overwhelming.

So, what was the target of popular furniture stores in the 80s? Simply put, it was to provide customers with a wide variety of furniture options and a personalized shopping experience. These stores were known for their trendy designs, quality materials, and attentive customer service.

To summarize, popular furniture stores in the 80s aimed to offer customers a unique shopping experience with a wide variety of trendy furniture options and exceptional customer service.

Ethan Allen

One of the most popular furniture stores in the 80s was Ethan Allen. This store was known for its classic and traditional furniture designs. I remember going to an Ethan Allen store with my parents as a child and being in awe of the beautiful pieces on display. From elegant dining room sets to cozy living room sofas, Ethan Allen had it all. They also offered a wide range of customization options, allowing customers to personalize their furniture to their liking.

Rooms To Go

Another popular furniture store in the 80s was Rooms To Go. This store was known for its affordable yet stylish furniture options. My family and I would often shop at Rooms To Go when we needed to furnish a new home or update our current furniture. They had everything from bedroom sets to living room sectionals, all at prices that were easy on the wallet.

The Importance of Furniture in the 80s

Furniture was an important aspect of 80s culture. It was a way for people to express their individuality and showcase their personal style. From bright and bold colors to sleek and modern designs, furniture was a reflection of the times. It was also a way for families to gather and spend time together. Whether it was a cozy movie night on the sofa or a formal dinner party around the dining table, furniture played a significant role in bringing people together.

Tips for Incorporating 80s Furniture into Your Home Today

If you're feeling nostalgic and want to incorporate some 80s furniture into your home today, here are some tips:

  • Look for classic and traditional designs, like those found at Ethan Allen.
  • Choose furniture with bright and bold colors to add a pop of retro flair to your space.
  • Don't be afraid to mix and match different styles and eras for a unique and eclectic look.
  • Consider incorporating vintage accents, like an old record player or rotary phone, to really capture the essence of the 80s.

Q&A

Q: What were some other popular furniture stores in the 80s?

A: Some other popular furniture stores in the 80s include Levitz Furniture, Thomasville Furniture, and La-Z-Boy.

Q: Was furniture more expensive in the 80s compared to today?

A: It's difficult to make a direct comparison since the cost of living and inflation rates have changed over time. However, it's safe to say that furniture prices have become more competitive in recent years due to the rise of online shopping and direct-to-consumer brands.

Q: What materials were commonly used in 80s furniture?

A: Materials such as glass, chrome, and mirrored surfaces were popular in 80s furniture. Additionally, pastel-colored fabrics and velour upholstery were also commonly used.

Q: Why do you think 80s furniture is making a comeback?

A: Nostalgia is a powerful thing, and many people are drawn to the bold and vibrant designs of the 80s. Additionally, there is a growing interest in sustainable and eco-friendly furniture, which often means incorporating vintage pieces into modern homes.

Conclusion of Popular Furniture Stores in the 80s

Although the 80s may seem like a distant memory, the furniture stores of that era left a lasting impression on many of us. From classic designs to affordable prices, these stores aimed to provide customers with a shopping experience that was both personalized and memorable. Whether you're a fan of retro style or simply looking for inspiration for your home, the popular furniture stores of the 80s are worth remembering.
