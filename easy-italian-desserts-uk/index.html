Classic Tiramisu Recipe

One of the most popular Italian desserts is Tiramisu. It is a layered dessert made with ladyfingers (savoiardi), mascarpone cheese, espresso, and cocoa powder. Here is a simple recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pound mascarpone cheese
  • 1/4 cup strong brewed coffee or espresso
  • 2 tablespoons rum
  • 24 ladyfingers
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder

Directions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together egg yolks and sugar until well blended. Whisk in milk and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
  2. Remove from heat and allow to cool.
  3. In a large bowl, beat cream with vanilla until stiff peaks form.
  4. Whisk mascarpone into yolk mixture until smooth and creamy.
  5. In a small bowl, combine coffee and rum.
  6. Dip each ladyfinger into coffee mixture and arrange in a single layer in a 9x13 inch dish.
  7. Spoon half of mascarpone mixture over ladyfingers, then repeat with another layer of ladyfingers and mascarpone mixture.
  8. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  9. Sprinkle cocoa powder over the top before serving.

Zeppole Recipe

Zeppole are a popular Italian dessert that are essentially deep-fried doughnuts. Here is a simple recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Directions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring water, butter, and salt to a boil over high heat.
  2. Reduce heat to low and add flour all at once, stirring vigorously with a wooden spoon until mixture forms a ball.
  3. Remove from heat and let cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition until mixture is smooth and glossy.
  5. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or heavy-bottomed pot to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  6. Using a cookie scoop or spoon, drop batter into hot oil and fry until golden brown, turning once.
  7. Drain on paper towels, then roll in sugar and cinnamon mixture.
