All Types Of Silk Fabric In India A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Jun 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Silk, also known as the queen of textiles, is a popular fabric in India. It is known for its luxurious feel, smooth texture, and durability. However, with so many different types of silk fabrics available in the market, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one. In this post, we will discuss all types of silk fabric in India and help you make an informed decision.

Are you struggling to choose between different types of silk fabrics? Do you want to know which silk fabric is the best for you? If so, keep reading.

When it comes to silk fabrics, there are numerous options available in the market. Each type of silk fabric has its unique characteristics, properties, and uses. Some of the most popular silk fabrics in India are:

1. Mulberry Silk

When we think of silk, the first type that comes to mind is generally mulberry silk. It is the most commonly used silk fabric in the world. Made from the cocoons of the silkworms of the Bombyx mori moth, this silk is known for its lustrous sheen, soft texture, and durability. Mulberry silk is often used in high-end fashion, such as sarees, lehengas, and dresses.

2. Tussar Silk

Tussar silk, also known as kosa silk, is a wild silk fabric. It is made from the cocoons of the Antheraea moth, which is found in the forests of India. This silk is known for its natural gold color, coarse texture, and light-weight. Tussar silk is often used in making sarees, suits, and jackets.

3. Muga Silk

Muga silk is a rare and expensive silk fabric. It is made from the cocoons of the Antheraea assamensis moth, which is found in the state of Assam. This silk is known for its durability, natural golden color, and high luster. Muga silk is often used in making sarees, mekhela chadors, and shawls.

4. Eri Silk

Eri silk, also known as endi or errandi silk, is a non-violent silk fabric. It is made from the cocoons of the Samia cynthia ricini moth, which is found in India and other parts of Asia. This silk is known for its soft texture, warmth, and durability. Eri silk is often used in making sarees, dresses, and shawls.

5. Kosa Silk

Kosa silk, also known as raw silk, is a textured silk fabric. It is made from the cocoons of the Antheraea paphia moth, which is found in India. This silk is known for its matte finish, rough texture, and durability. Kosa silk is often used in making sarees, suits, and jackets.

6. Bhagalpuri Silk

Bhagalpuri silk, also known as tussar silk, is a handloom silk fabric. It is made in the town of Bhagalpur in Bihar. This silk is known for its unique texture, durability, and affordability. Bhagalpuri silk is often used in making sarees, suits, and dupattas.

Now that you know about the different types of silk fabric in India let's move on to some frequently asked questions.

Question and Answer

Q. What is the most expensive silk fabric in India?

A. Muga silk is the most expensive silk fabric in India.

Q. Which silk fabric is the softest?

A. Mulberry silk is considered to be the softest silk fabric.

Q. Which silk fabric is best for summer?

A. Eri silk is a good option for summer as it is light-weight and breathable.

Q. Which silk fabric is best for winter?

A. Muga silk is a good option for winter as it is warm and durable.

Conclusion of All Types of Silk Fabric in India

Choosing the right silk fabric is important as it can make or break your outfit. We hope that this comprehensive guide to all types of silk fabric in India has helped you understand the different options available in the market. Whether you are looking for a soft, lustrous fabric or a coarse, textured one, there is a silk fabric for every occasion.
