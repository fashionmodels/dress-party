The dhoti-kurta is a two-piece attire, with the dhoti being a long piece of cloth wrapped around the waist and legs, and the kurta being a long shirt worn over it. Popular National Costumes of India

There are several popular national costumes of India, each with its unique style and design. Some of the most famous national costumes of India include:

1. Saree

The saree is a long piece of cloth draped around the body and is worn with a blouse and petticoat. It is the most popular national costume of India for women and is worn on special occasions like weddings, festivals, and religious ceremonies.

2. Dhoti-Kurta

The dhoti-kurta is a two-piece attire, with the dhoti being a long piece of cloth wrapped around the waist and legs, and the kurta being a long shirt worn over it. It is the most popular national costume of India for men and is worn on special occasions like weddings, festivals, and religious ceremonies.

3. Salwar Kameez

The salwar kameez is a two or three-piece attire that consists of a long tunic, loose pants, and a scarf. It is a popular national costume of India for women and is worn on a daily basis in some parts of the country.

4. Sherwani

The sherwani is a long coat-like attire that is worn over the dhoti or churidar pants. It is a popular national costume of India for men and is worn on special occasions like weddings and religious ceremonies.

How to Wear National Costume of India?

The national costume of India is easy to wear and requires no special skills. However, it is essential to follow the correct draping technique to ensure that the attire looks elegant and comfortable. Here are some simple steps to wear the national costume of India:

1. Saree

To wear a saree, start by tucking one end of the saree into the petticoat at the waist. Then, wrap the saree around the waist once and bring it to the front, pleating it as you go. Tuck the pleats into the petticoat and drape the remaining fabric over the left shoulder. Pin the saree to the blouse to keep it in place.

2. Dhoti-Kurta

To wear a dhoti-kurta, start by wearing the kurta like a shirt over your pants. Then, take the dhoti and tuck one end of it into the waistband at the back. Bring the other end to the front and tuck it in, making sure that the dhoti is pleated. Finally, tuck the pleats into the waistband and adjust the dhoti as required.
