What is the Significance of Maang Tikka?

Maang Tikka is not just a piece of jewelry but is also a symbol of Indian culture and tradition. It is an essential part of bridal jewelry and is worn by the bride on her wedding day. The Maang Tikka is placed on the bride's forehead, which is considered the sixth chakra in Indian tradition. It is believed that the sixth chakra is the center of intuition, wisdom, and spiritual insight. When the Maang Tikka is placed on the forehead, it is said to activate the sixth chakra, which helps the bride to connect with her inner self.

As per Indian tradition, the Maang Tikka also symbolizes the union of two souls in marriage. It is believed that the chain that runs down the hairline represents the groom's bond with the bride. The Maang Tikka is also considered a sign of good fortune and is believed to bring prosperity and happiness to the newlyweds.
