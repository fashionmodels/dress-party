What Colour Do Hindu Brides Wear

Written by Aprilia Jun 30, 2023 · 5 min read

Weddings are one of the most significant events in a person's life. It is a day when the bride and groom come together to celebrate their love and commitment to each other. For Hindu brides, choosing the right wedding attire, especially the colour of their wedding dress, can be a daunting task.

Hindu brides face several challenges when it comes to selecting the perfect colour for their wedding dress. The colour needs to be auspicious, symbolise purity, and be in line with their cultural and religious beliefs. Additionally, the bride's dress needs to complement her skin tone, body type, and personal style.

The traditional colour for Hindu brides is red. The colour has a symbolic meaning as it represents love, passion, fertility, and prosperity. In Hindu culture, red is believed to ward off evil spirits and bring good luck to the couple. Red is by far the most popular colour for Hindu brides, and it is the colour that most people associate with Hindu weddings.

In recent years, brides have started to experiment with other colours such as pink, peach, gold, and green. These colours are still considered to be auspicious and symbolise purity and prosperity. The choice of colour often depends on the bride's personal preference, the season, and the wedding theme.

Personal Experience and Explanation of Red Colour

As a Hindu, I always knew that I wanted to wear a red wedding dress. Red has a special place in my heart as it is the colour of love, passion, and commitment. When I was shopping for my wedding dress, I tried on many different colours, but none of them felt right. It wasn't until I put on the red dress that I knew it was the one. The colour made me feel confident, beautiful, and connected to my culture and traditions.

Red is not just a colour; it is a symbol of love and commitment. When a Hindu bride wears a red dress, she is making a statement about her love for her partner and her commitment to the marriage. Red is also a symbol of fertility and prosperity, and it is believed to bring good luck to the couple.

Choosing the Right Colour for Your Wedding Dress

Choosing the right colour for your wedding dress can be a daunting task. Here are some tips to help you choose the perfect colour:

Consider Your Skin Tone

The colour of your dress should complement your skin tone. If you have a warm skin tone, go for colours like gold, peach, and red. If you have a cool skin tone, choose colours like blue, green, and pink.

Consider Your Body Type

The colour of your dress should also complement your body type. If you have a fuller figure, choose colours that are darker and more subdued. If you are petite, opt for lighter colours that will make you appear taller.

Personal Experience and Explanation of Pink Colour

When my sister got married, she decided to wear a pink wedding dress. Pink is a beautiful colour that symbolises love, compassion, and nurturing. It is a softer alternative to the traditional red dress, and it complemented my sister's fair skin tone perfectly. The pink dress looked elegant and sophisticated, and it added a touch of femininity to her wedding attire.

Conclusion of What Colour Do Hindu Brides Wear

Choosing the right colour for your wedding dress can be a challenging task, especially for Hindu brides. Red is the traditional colour for Hindu brides, and it symbolises love, passion, fertility, and prosperity. However, in recent years, brides have started to experiment with other colours such as pink, peach, gold, and green. The choice of colour often depends on the bride's personal preference, the season, and the wedding theme. When choosing the perfect colour for your wedding dress, consider your skin tone, body type, and personal style. Whatever colour you choose, make sure it makes you feel confident, beautiful, and connected to your culture and traditions.

Question and Answer

Q: Can Hindu brides wear white wedding dresses?

A: In Hindu culture, white is the colour of mourning, and it is not typically worn at weddings. However, some brides may choose to wear white or ivory dresses as they are considered to be elegant and timeless.

Q: Can Hindu brides wear black wedding dresses?

A: Black is not traditionally worn at Hindu weddings as it is associated with death and mourning. However, some brides may choose to wear black dresses as they are considered to be elegant and sophisticated.

Q: Can Hindu brides wear colourful wedding dresses?

A: Yes, Hindu brides can wear colourful wedding dresses. In recent years, brides have started to experiment with different colours such as pink, peach, gold, and green. The choice of colour often depends on the bride's personal preference, the season, and the wedding theme.

Q: What does the colour of a Hindu groom's attire symbolise?

A: The colour of a Hindu groom's attire can vary depending on the region and the community. However, in general, the groom's attire is usually a shade of beige or cream, which symbolises purity, simplicity, and elegance.
