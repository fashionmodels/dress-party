Question and Answer

Q: How do I know if a boot fits properly?

A: When trying on boots, be sure to wear the socks you plan on wearing during your outdoor activities. Your boots should feel snug but not tight, with enough room to wiggle your toes. Walk around in them for a bit to ensure they feel comfortable and supportive.

Q: What if I have wide feet?

A: Look for boots that come in wider sizes or have adjustable laces or straps. Don't try to squeeze into a pair of boots that are too narrow, as this can cause discomfort and even injury.

Q: How often should I replace my boots?

A: This depends on how often you use them and the conditions you expose them to. As a general rule, you should replace your boots every 300-500 miles of use or every 2-3 years.

Q: Can I wear my hiking boots for other activities?

A: While hiking boots are designed specifically for outdoor activities, they can also be worn for other activities that require sturdy and supportive footwear, such as yard work or construction.
