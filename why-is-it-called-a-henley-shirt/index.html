Why Is It Called A Henley Shirt

Written by Frank Sep 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered why a certain type of shirt is called a henley? You may have heard of this shirt style, but you’re not sure what it really means. In this blog post, we’ll explore the history and origins of the henley shirt and why it’s called that way.

If you’re a fashion enthusiast or simply curious about clothing, you may have come across the term “henley shirt” before. However, you might not know what distinguishes it from other shirt styles. You might also be wondering why it’s called a henley shirt in the first place.

The henley shirt is named after the English town of Henley-on-Thames, which is located in Oxfordshire. The shirt style originated from the traditional rowing uniforms worn by the rowers competing in the annual Henley Royal Regatta, a prestigious rowing event that has been held since 1839. Henley shirts were initially made with a collarless design and a buttoned placket, which made them more practical for rowers.

In summary, a henley shirt is a collarless shirt with a buttoned placket that was originally worn by rowers in the Henley Royal Regatta. It was named after the town of Henley-on-Thames, where the regatta is held annually.

The History of the Henley Shirt

My first experience with a henley shirt was when I saw my grandfather wearing one. I remember being fascinated by the unique collarless design and the buttoned placket. He told me that it was a practical shirt for rowers, and that’s why it was called a henley.

The history of the henley shirt dates back to the late 1800s, when it was worn as a uniform by English rowers. These rowers needed a practical shirt design that would allow them to move freely and comfortably while rowing. The collarless design of the henley prevented chafing, while the buttoned placket allowed for ventilation.

The Anatomy of a Henley Shirt

A henley shirt is a collarless shirt with a buttoned placket that extends about halfway down the chest. It typically has three buttons, although some styles may have more or fewer buttons.
