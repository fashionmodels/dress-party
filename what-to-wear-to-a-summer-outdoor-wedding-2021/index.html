What To Wear To A Summer Outdoor Wedding 2021 The Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Summer is the perfect season for weddings, but with the warm weather comes the challenge of deciding what to wear to a summer outdoor wedding 2021. You want to look stylish, but also stay comfortable in the heat. Finding the perfect outfit can be overwhelming, but we're here to help!

Attending a wedding can be stressful enough, and worrying about what to wear only adds to the pressure. You don't want to underdress or overdress, and you also want to follow the dress code. But don't worry, we've got you covered!

Understanding the Dress Code

The first step in deciding what to wear to a summer outdoor wedding 2021 is understanding the dress code. The dress code is usually mentioned in the invitation, so make sure to read it carefully. If the dress code is not mentioned, don't hesitate to ask the bride or groom.

If the dress code is casual, you can opt for a sundress or a maxi dress. If the dress code is semi-formal or formal, you can go for a cocktail dress or a long dress. For men, a casual dress code means khakis and a button-down shirt, while a formal dress code means a suit and tie.

Choosing the Right Fabric

Choosing the right fabric is crucial when it comes to what to wear to a summer outdoor wedding 2021. You want to stay cool and comfortable, so lightweight fabrics such as cotton, linen, and chiffon are your best bet. Avoid heavy fabrics such as velvet or wool.

Another thing to consider when choosing the fabric is the color. Dark colors absorb more heat, so it's best to opt for light colors such as pastels, whites, and creams. You can also go for bright colors and bold prints, but make sure they are appropriate for the occasion and the dress code.

Accessorizing Your Outfit

Accessorizing your outfit is just as important as choosing the right dress or suit. You can add a pop of color with your jewelry or shoes, but make sure to keep it classy and elegant. Avoid wearing anything too flashy or distracting.

For women, a small clutch or a crossbody bag is the perfect accessory to carry your essentials. For men, a sleek leather wallet is all you need. Don't forget to wear comfortable shoes, as you'll be on your feet for most of the day.

Tips for Men

For men wondering what to wear to a summer outdoor wedding 2021, a lightweight suit in a light color such as beige or gray is a great choice. Pair it with a white shirt and a tie, and you're good to go. You can also opt for a blazer and khakis for a more casual look.

When it comes to shoes, loafers or dress shoes in a light color such as brown or tan are the perfect choice. Avoid wearing sneakers or sandals, as they are too casual for a wedding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I wear black to a summer outdoor wedding?

A: While black is a classic color, it's best to avoid it for a summer outdoor wedding. Opt for lighter colors such as pastels, whites, and creams. If you must wear black, make sure to add some color with your accessories.

Q: Can I wear shorts to a summer outdoor wedding?

A: No, shorts are not appropriate for a wedding, even if it's a casual dress code. Opt for a sundress or a maxi dress instead.

Q: Can I wear a hat to a summer outdoor wedding?

A: It depends on the dress code and the style of the hat. If it's a formal dress code, skip the hat. If it's a casual dress code, you can wear a floppy hat or a fedora, but make sure it matches your outfit.

Q: Can I wear a jumpsuit to a summer outdoor wedding?

A: Yes, a jumpsuit is a great alternative to a dress for a summer outdoor wedding, as long as it's not too casual. Opt for a jumpsuit in a lightweight fabric and a bright color or print.

Conclusion

Choosing what to wear to a summer outdoor wedding 2021 can be overwhelming, but it doesn't have to be. Understanding the dress code, choosing the right fabric and accessories, and following our tips will ensure you look stylish and stay comfortable in the heat. Remember to always check the dress code and don't be afraid to ask the bride or groom if you're unsure. Happy wedding season!
