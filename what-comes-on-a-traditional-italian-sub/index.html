The Ultimate Guide To What Comes On A Traditional Italian Sub

Written by Oliver Oct 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of ordering the same sandwich every time you go to a deli? Do you want to try something new and authentic? Look no further than the traditional Italian sub. Packed with flavor and a variety of ingredients, this sandwich is a favorite among sandwich lovers everywhere.

Have you ever found yourself overwhelmed by the options available when ordering a sandwich? Are you unsure about what comes on a traditional Italian sub? Do you want to know how to order it to your liking? This guide will answer all of your questions and more.

The traditional Italian sub is a sandwich that typically includes a variety of deli meats such as salami, pepperoni, and ham, as well as provolone cheese, lettuce, tomatoes, onions, oil and vinegar, and sometimes hot peppers. It is usually served on a long roll or baguette.

In summary, a traditional Italian sub typically includes deli meats, cheese, vegetables, oil and vinegar, and hot peppers (optional) served on a long roll or baguette.

What is on a Traditional Italian Sub and Why You'll Love It

As an Italian-American, I grew up eating traditional Italian subs, and they have always been one of my favorite sandwiches. The combination of meats, cheese, and vegetables, all drizzled in oil and vinegar, creates a delicious explosion of flavors in your mouth.

The variety of ingredients in a traditional Italian sub makes it an exciting sandwich to eat. You never know which bite will have a piece of spicy pepperoni or a slice of refreshing tomato.

How to Customize Your Traditional Italian Sub

While the traditional Italian sub comes with a standard set of ingredients, you can always customize it to your liking. If you prefer turkey over ham, ask for it to be substituted. If you don't like spicy food, skip the hot peppers. The beauty of the traditional Italian sub is that it can be tailored to your taste preferences.

The Importance of the Roll/Bread in a Traditional Italian Sub

One of the most important components of a traditional Italian sub is the roll or bread. It should be crusty on the outside and soft on the inside, allowing the ingredients to meld together and create a perfect bite. If the bread is stale or too soft, it can ruin the overall sandwich experience.

The Best Meats and Cheeses for a Traditional Italian Sub

While the standard deli meats used in a traditional Italian sub are delicious, there are other options to consider. Prosciutto and mortadella are popular choices that add a unique flavor to the sandwich. As for cheese, provolone is the classic choice, but mozzarella or even asiago can be used for a twist on the traditional.

My Favorite Variation of the Traditional Italian Sub

My personal favorite variation of the traditional Italian sub is adding roasted red peppers. The sweetness of the peppers complements the saltiness of the meats and cheese perfectly.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I add mayo to my traditional Italian sub?

A: While mayo is not a typical ingredient in a traditional Italian sub, you can certainly add it if you prefer it.

Q: Can I order a traditional Italian sub without any vegetables?

A: Yes, you can customize your sandwich to your preferences, but keep in mind that the vegetables add a refreshing crunch to the sandwich.

Q: Is a traditional Italian sub typically served hot or cold?

A: A traditional Italian sub is typically served cold, but some variations include hot ingredients such as meatballs or sausage.

Q: What type of bread is typically used for a traditional Italian sub?

A: A long roll or baguette is the typical bread used for a traditional Italian sub.

Conclusion of What Comes on a Traditional Italian Sub

The traditional Italian sub is a classic sandwich that is loved by many for its combination of deli meats, cheese, and vegetables. While it comes with a standard set of ingredients, it can be customized to your liking. Don't be afraid to try new variations and add your own twist to this beloved sandwich.
