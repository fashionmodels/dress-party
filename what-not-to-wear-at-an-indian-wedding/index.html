What Not To Wear At An Indian Wedding A Guide To Avoid Fashion Faux Pas

Written by Frank Jul 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Indian weddings are known for their vibrant colors, elaborate decorations, and rich cultural traditions. As a guest, you want to make sure that you dress appropriately to show respect for the couple and their families. However, with so many different types of Indian weddings and dress codes, it can be challenging to know what to wear. In this guide, we will explore what not to wear at an Indian wedding to help you avoid any fashion faux pas.

Attending an Indian wedding can be overwhelming, especially if you are not familiar with the culture or traditions. You want to make sure that you dress appropriately, but you also want to make sure that you do not offend anyone or stand out for the wrong reasons. Understanding what not to wear at an Indian wedding can help alleviate some of the stress and anxiety that comes with attending such an event.

When it comes to what not to wear at an Indian wedding, there are a few things to keep in mind. Firstly, avoid wearing white or black as these colors are traditionally associated with mourning and funerals. Secondly, avoid wearing revealing or tight-fitting clothing as Indian weddings are typically modest and conservative affairs. Lastly, avoid wearing shoes that are uncomfortable or difficult to walk in as Indian weddings can last for several hours.

In summary, when attending an Indian wedding, it is important to dress appropriately and respectfully. This means avoiding certain colors and clothing styles that may be deemed inappropriate or offensive. By following these guidelines, you can ensure that you have a memorable and enjoyable experience at the wedding.

Avoid Wearing White or Black

During an Indian wedding, it is customary to wear bright, bold colors that represent joy and happiness. White and black, on the other hand, are traditionally associated with mourning and funerals. Therefore, wearing these colors to an Indian wedding may be seen as disrespectful or inappropriate.

Personally, I made the mistake of wearing a black dress to an Indian wedding. I thought it was a safe choice because black is a classic color that goes with everything. However, when I arrived at the wedding, I noticed that everyone was wearing bright, vibrant colors. I felt out of place and wished I had chosen a more festive outfit.

When choosing what to wear to an Indian wedding, opt for colors such as red, pink, green, and gold. These colors are considered auspicious and are commonly worn by the bride and groom as well as the wedding guests.

Avoid Revealing or Tight-Fitting Clothing

Indian weddings are typically modest and conservative affairs. Therefore, it is important to avoid wearing clothing that is too revealing or tight-fitting. This includes short skirts, low-cut tops, and tight dresses. Instead, opt for clothing that is elegant, modest, and comfortable.

For women, a popular outfit choice for an Indian wedding is a saree or a salwar kameez. These outfits are elegant, modest, and come in a variety of colors and styles. For men, a traditional outfit such as a sherwani or a kurta pajama is a good choice.

Avoid Uncomfortable Shoes

Indian weddings can last for several hours, so it is important to wear comfortable shoes that you can walk and dance in. Avoid wearing high heels or shoes that are too tight or uncomfortable. Instead, opt for shoes that are comfortable, stylish, and easy to walk in.

Personally, I made the mistake of wearing high heels to an Indian wedding. While they looked great, my feet were aching after just a few hours of standing and dancing. I wished I had worn a pair of comfortable flats or sandals instead.

Avoid Overdoing the Jewelry

Indian weddings are known for their elaborate jewelry and accessories. However, it is important to avoid overdoing it when it comes to jewelry. Opt for a few statement pieces that complement your outfit rather than wearing too much jewelry that can be distracting.

For women, a statement necklace or a pair of earrings can add a touch of elegance to your outfit. For men, a simple watch or a pair of cufflinks can add a touch of sophistication to your outfit.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear Western clothing to an Indian wedding?

A: While it is not traditional to wear Western clothing to an Indian wedding, it is becoming more common. However, it is important to keep in mind the dress code and the cultural traditions of the wedding. If in doubt, it is always better to err on the side of caution and wear traditional Indian clothing.

Q: Can I wear jeans to an Indian wedding?

A: No, jeans are not appropriate for an Indian wedding. Indian weddings are typically formal affairs, and guests are expected to dress accordingly. Opt for traditional Indian clothing or formal Western clothing such as a suit or a dress.

Q: Can I wear sneakers to an Indian wedding?

A: No, sneakers are not appropriate for an Indian wedding. Opt for formal shoes such as loafers or dress shoes that are comfortable and easy to walk in.

Q: Can I wear a sleeveless dress to an Indian wedding?

A: It is best to avoid wearing sleeveless dresses to an Indian wedding as they are considered too revealing. Opt for a dress with sleeves or wear a shawl or jacket to cover your arms.

Conclusion of What Not to Wear at an Indian Wedding

Attending an Indian wedding can be a memorable and enjoyable experience. However, it is important to dress appropriately and respectfully. Avoid wearing white or black, revealing or tight-fitting clothing, uncomfortable shoes, and overdoing the jewelry. By following these guidelines, you can ensure that you have a memorable and enjoyable experience at the wedding.
