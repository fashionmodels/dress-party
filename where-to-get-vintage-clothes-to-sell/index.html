Where To Get Vintage Clothes To Sell A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jul 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to start a vintage clothing business but don't know where to get your inventory from? Or maybe you're already selling vintage clothes but want to expand your selection. Whatever the case, finding quality vintage pieces to sell can be a challenge. In this guide, we'll explore the best places to source vintage clothing for resale, and give you tips on how to find the best pieces.

As a vintage seller, you may already be aware of the challenges of finding quality vintage pieces to sell. The hunt for vintage clothes can be time-consuming, and there's no guarantee that the pieces you find will be in good condition or sellable. It can also be a challenge to find a variety of sizes and styles that will appeal to your customers. But don't worry, we've got you covered.

The first place to start when looking for vintage clothes to sell is thrift stores. These stores are a treasure trove of vintage clothing, and you never know what you'll find. You can also try estate sales, garage sales, and flea markets. These are all great places to find vintage clothing, but you need to be prepared to do some digging.

Online Marketplaces

Another great place to find vintage clothing to sell is online marketplaces. Sites like Etsy, Depop, and eBay are all great places to find vintage clothing. You can also try social media platforms like Instagram and Facebook. Many vintage sellers have their own Instagram accounts where they sell their inventory.

When searching for vintage clothing online, it's important to do your research. Look for reputable sellers with good feedback and quality items. Be sure to ask questions about the condition of the clothing and any flaws. It's also important to check the measurements of the clothing, as vintage sizing can be different than modern sizing.

Wholesale Vintage Clothing Suppliers

If you're looking to buy vintage clothing in bulk, you may want to consider working with a wholesale vintage clothing supplier. These suppliers specialize in sourcing vintage clothing and selling it to retailers. Some popular wholesale vintage clothing suppliers include Beyond Retro Wholesale, Ragstock Wholesale, and Dust Factory.

Working with a wholesale vintage clothing supplier can be a great way to build your inventory quickly. However, it's important to do your research and make sure you're working with a reputable supplier. Look for reviews and ask for references before making a purchase.

Local Vintage Stores and Markets

Finally, don't forget about local vintage stores and markets. These are great places to find unique pieces that you won't find anywhere else. Local vintage stores often have a curated selection of vintage clothing, and the owners may be able to help you find specific pieces. Vintage markets are also a great way to connect with other vintage sellers and find new sources for inventory.

Tips for Finding the Best Vintage Clothing

Now that you know where to find vintage clothing to sell, here are a few tips to help you find the best pieces:

  • Look for high-quality materials like silk, wool, and cashmere.
  • Check for any damage or stains, and be prepared to do some repairs.
  • Look for unique pieces that will stand out in your inventory.
  • Consider the current fashion trends and what your customers are looking for.

Conclusion of Where to Get Vintage Clothes to Sell

Sourcing vintage clothing for resale can be a challenge, but with the right resources and strategies, you can build a successful vintage clothing business. Whether you're scouring thrift stores, shopping online, or working with wholesale suppliers, there are plenty of options for finding quality vintage pieces to sell. Remember to do your research, ask questions, and don't be afraid to negotiate prices. Happy hunting!

Question and Answer

Q: What's the best place to find high-end vintage clothing?

A: Vintage stores and markets are often the best places to find high-end vintage clothing. You can also try online marketplaces like The RealReal and Vestiaire Collective.

Q: How do I know if a vintage piece is worth buying?

A: Look for high-quality materials, unique details, and good condition. Consider the brand and designer, and whether the piece is in line with current fashion trends.

Q: Can I negotiate prices when buying vintage clothing?

A: Yes, many vintage sellers are open to negotiation, especially if you're buying multiple items. Just be respectful and don't lowball them.

Q: How do I know if a wholesale vintage clothing supplier is reputable?

A: Look for reviews and references, and ask for samples before making a large purchase. You can also check if they're a member of the Vintage Fashion Guild or National Association of Vintage Clothing and Textile Dealers.
