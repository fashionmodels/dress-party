How Much Do Professional Boxers Make Starting Out

Written by Bardi Jun 01, 2023 · 4 min read

Boxing is a physically demanding sport that requires years of training and dedication to reach the professional level. However, one common question that aspiring boxers often ask is, “How much do professional boxers make starting out?” This is a valid concern since most boxers start their careers with limited exposure and experience, and it can be challenging to earn a living from boxing alone.

Boxing is a highly competitive sport, and professional boxers starting out may face numerous challenges, including low pay, limited opportunities to fight, and a lack of recognition. The life of a professional boxer can be financially challenging, especially in the early stages of their career.

Professional boxers starting out can expect to earn less than established boxers. The amount of money that a professional boxer makes starting out depends on several factors, including their level of experience, weight class, and the quality of their opponents.

In summary, professional boxers starting out can expect to earn less than established boxers. The amount of money a boxer makes depends on various factors, including their level of experience, weight class, and the quality of their opponents.

What is the Average Starting Salary for a Professional Boxer?

As mentioned earlier, the amount of money that professional boxers starting out can expect to earn varies significantly, depending on various factors. However, according to a recent report by Forbes, the average salary for a professional boxer is around $35,584 per year. This amount is considerably low compared to other professional athletes, such as basketball, football and baseball players.

However, it is essential to note that this is just an average, and some boxers can earn less or much more than this amount, depending on their performance, endorsements, and other factors.

Personal Experience of a Professional Boxer Starting Out

As a professional boxer starting out, I can attest that the journey can be challenging financially. It took me several years of training and fighting in local and regional tournaments to get my professional boxing license. When I started fighting professionally, I earned as little as $1,000 per fight, which was barely enough to cover my training and living expenses.

However, as I gained more exposure and experience, my earnings increased, and I started getting better-paying fights, endorsements, and sponsorships. Today, I make a decent living from boxing, but I still have to train hard, stay focused, and perform well in every fight to maintain my position in the sport.

How to Increase Your Earnings as a Professional Boxer Starting Out

Professional boxers starting out can increase their earnings by following these tips:

1. Build Your Reputation

Boxers with a good reputation and a strong record of wins are more likely to get better-paying fights and endorsements. Therefore, it's essential to train hard, stay focused, and perform well in every fight to build your reputation as a professional boxer.

2. Network

Networking is essential in the boxing industry. Attend boxing events, meet other boxers, coaches, and promoters, and build strong relationships with them. This can open up more opportunities for you to fight, get endorsements, and sponsorships.

3. Get a Good Manager

A good manager can help you negotiate better deals, get better-paying fights, and endorsements. Therefore, it's essential to find a reputable manager who understands the boxing industry and can help you succeed.

4. Stay Focused on Your Goals

Boxing is a challenging sport, and it's easy to get distracted or discouraged when things don't go as planned. However, it's essential to stay focused on your goals and keep working hard to achieve them. This can help you increase your earnings and achieve success in the sport.
