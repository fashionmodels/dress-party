What is the Famous Dress of Sikkim?

The traditional dress of Sikkim is known as ‘Bakhu’ for women and ‘Bakhuwa’ for men. It is a full-length cloak-like garment made from thick woolen fabric, which is mostly handwoven. The Bakhu is usually worn over a full-sleeved blouse called ‘Honju’ and a skirt called ‘Dokhna’ that reaches the ankles. Women also wear a colorful scarf called ‘Tharo’ around their necks, which adds a pop of color to the outfit.

Men wear a Bakhuwa over a full-sleeved shirt and trousers, which are also made of woolen fabric. They also wear a cap called ‘Bakhi’, which is made from the same fabric as the Bakhuwa.

The Bakhu and Bakhuwa are available in a variety of colors and designs, with each design representing a different community or region of Sikkim. The traditional dress of Sikkim is not only worn during festivals and celebrations but is also a part of everyday clothing for many people in the region.
