Fashion Designer Salary Per Month Philippines How Much Can You Expect To Earn

Written by Luffy Oct 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a budding fashion designer in the Philippines wondering about the average salary in the industry? Perhaps you’re already working in the field and wondering if you’re being paid fairly. Either way, understanding fashion designer salary per month in the Philippines can be a tricky business, and it’s important to have a realistic view of what you can expect to earn.

The average salary for a fashion designer in the Philippines varies greatly, depending on factors such as experience, location, and the type of employer you work for. However, many designers in the country do face common challenges when it comes to earning a stable income and advancing in their careers.

So, what can you expect to earn as a fashion designer in the Philippines? According to SalaryExplorer, the average monthly salary for a fashion designer in the country is around PHP 28,000. However, this can range from as low as PHP 14,600 to as high as PHP 46,700 per month depending on the factors mentioned above.

To break it down further, designers working in the fashion and retail sectors tend to earn less than those employed in luxury fashion or high-end design firms. Freelance designers may also earn less than those working for a company, but they have more flexibility and control over their schedules and projects.

My Experience as a Fashion Designer in the Philippines

As a fashion designer myself, I can attest to the challenges of earning a stable income in this field in the Philippines. When I first started out, I was working for a small fashion house in Manila and earning around PHP 18,000 per month. While this was enough to cover my basic expenses, it was difficult to save money or invest in my future as a designer.

However, as I gained more experience and built my portfolio, I was able to negotiate for higher pay and better opportunities. Today, I work as a freelance designer and earn an average of PHP 35,000 per month, with some months bringing in even more depending on the projects I take on.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Fashion Designer in the Philippines

If you’re looking to increase your salary as a fashion designer in the Philippines, there are a few steps you can take. Firstly, focus on building your skills and portfolio by taking classes, attending workshops, and networking with other designers and industry professionals.

You can also consider specialising in a particular niche or area of design, such as bridal wear or sustainable fashion. This can help you stand out from the competition and command higher rates for your services.

Finally, don’t be afraid to negotiate for higher pay or seek out new opportunities if you feel that your current job isn’t meeting your financial needs. It’s important to value your skills and experience and advocate for yourself in the industry.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the average fashion designer salary per month in the Philippines?

According to SalaryExplorer, the average monthly salary for a fashion designer in the Philippines is around PHP 28,000.

2. What factors affect fashion designer salary in the Philippines?

The factors that affect fashion designer salary in the Philippines include experience, location, and the type of employer you work for.

3. Can freelance fashion designers earn more than those working for a company?

While freelance designers may earn less than those working for a company, they have more flexibility and control over their schedules and projects, which can sometimes lead to higher pay and better opportunities.

4. How can I increase my salary as a fashion designer in the Philippines?

To increase your salary as a fashion designer in the Philippines, focus on building your skills and portfolio, consider specialising in a particular niche or area of design, and don’t be afraid to negotiate for higher pay or seek out new opportunities.

Conclusion of Fashion Designer Salary per Month Philippines

While the average salary for a fashion designer in the Philippines may not be as high as in other countries, there are still opportunities to earn a good living and build a successful career in the industry. By building your skills, specialising in a niche area, and advocating for yourself in the industry, you can increase your chances of earning a higher salary and achieving your goals as a fashion designer in the Philippines.
