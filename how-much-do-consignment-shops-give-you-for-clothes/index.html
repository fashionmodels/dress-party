How Much Do Consignment Shops Give You For Clothes

Written by Oliver Sep 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your old clothes and looking to make some extra cash? Consignment shops might be the answer to your problem. However, before you start packing up your clothes and heading to the nearest consignment store, you might be wondering, "how much do consignment shops give you for clothes?"

The truth is, it varies from store to store. Some consignment shops give you a higher percentage of the profits while others may only offer a flat rate per item. It's important to do your research and compare different consignment stores to find the best deal for your clothes.

Typically, consignment shops give you around 30-40% of the final selling price of your clothes. For example, if your item sells for $100, you can expect to receive $30-$40. However, this percentage can vary depending on the store's policies and the demand for your items.

In summary, how much consignment shops give you for clothes depends on the store's policies and the demand for your items. It's important to do your research and compare different stores to find the best deal for your clothes.

Factors Affecting How Much Consignment Shops Give You for Clothes

When I first started selling my clothes at consignment shops, I was surprised to find out that the amount I received varied from store to store. After some research, I found out that there are various factors that affect how much consignment shops give you for clothes. Here are some of them:

1. Condition of Your Clothes

Consignment shops typically only accept clothes that are in good condition. If your clothes are in excellent condition, you can expect to receive a higher percentage of the profits. However, if your clothes are damaged or have stains, the consignment shop may not accept them or offer a lower price.

2. Brand Name

Brand name clothes tend to sell for more at consignment shops, which means you can expect to receive a higher percentage of the profits. However, if your clothes are from an unknown brand, you may not receive as much.

3. Season and Trends

The season and current fashion trends can also affect how much consignment shops give you for clothes. For example, if you're selling winter clothes in the summer, you may not receive as much because there is less demand for them.

4. Location

The location of the consignment shop can also affect how much you receive for your clothes. Shops in high-end neighborhoods tend to offer a higher percentage of the profits because they cater to a wealthier clientele.

Question and Answer

Q: How do I find the best consignment shop for my clothes?

A: You can start by researching different consignment shops in your area and comparing their policies and percentage of profits. You can also ask for recommendations from friends or family who have sold their clothes at consignment shops before.

Q: How should I prepare my clothes for consignment?

A: Make sure your clothes are clean and in good condition. Remove any stains or damages, and iron or steam them if necessary. It's also a good idea to bring your clothes on hangers or neatly folded in a box or bag.

Q: How long does it take for my clothes to sell at a consignment shop?

A: It depends on the demand for your items and the policies of the store. Some consignment shops may only keep your clothes for a certain period of time before returning them to you or donating them to charity.

Q: Can I negotiate the percentage of profits with a consignment shop?

A: It depends on the store's policies. Some consignment shops may be open to negotiating the percentage of profits, while others may have a strict policy in place.

Conclusion of How Much Do Consignment Shops Give You for Clothes

Now that you know the factors affecting how much consignment shops give you for clothes, you can make an informed decision on where to sell your clothes. Remember to do your research and compare different stores to find the best deal for your clothes. With a little effort, you can turn your old clothes into cash and make room for new ones in your wardrobe.
