How To Sell Your Wedding Dress At Consignment Shops

Written by Aprilia Aug 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you wondering what to do with your wedding dress now that the big day has come and gone? Many brides struggle with this decision, as wedding dresses can be expensive and hold sentimental value. One option to consider is selling your dress at a consignment shop. In this post, we will explore the process of consignment shops buying wedding dresses and how it can benefit you.

It can be challenging to let go of a cherished wedding dress, but it can also take up valuable closet space and collect dust over time. Selling your dress at a consignment shop can be a great way to free up space and make some money in the process. However, it can also be intimidating to navigate the consignment process and ensure that you are getting a fair price for your dress.

The target of consignment shops buying wedding dresses is to provide brides with a platform to sell their dresses at a reasonable price while also offering potential buyers a more affordable option to purchase a beautiful wedding dress.

In summary, consignment shops buy wedding dresses to provide a marketplace for brides to sell their dresses and for potential buyers to purchase a wedding dress at a more reasonable price. This process can be beneficial for both parties involved.

Selling Your Dress at a Consignment Shop

When I got married, I struggled with what to do with my wedding dress. It was a beautiful dress that I had spent a lot of money on, but I knew I would never wear it again. I eventually decided to sell it at a consignment shop, and it was one of the best decisions I made.

The process of selling my dress at a consignment shop was straightforward. First, I researched local consignment shops that specialized in wedding dresses. Then, I contacted the shops to see if they were currently accepting dresses and what their commission rates were. Once I found a shop that I was comfortable with, I brought my dress in for them to evaluate.

The shop evaluated my dress and determined a fair price to sell it for. They took professional photos of the dress and added it to their inventory. When the dress sold, I received a percentage of the sale price, and the consignment shop kept the rest as their commission.

Finding the Right Consignment Shop

When looking for a consignment shop to sell your wedding dress, it is important to do your research. Look for a shop that specializes in wedding dresses and has a good reputation in the community. You can also ask for recommendations from friends or family members who have sold their dresses at consignment shops.

When contacting the shop, ask about their commission rates, how they evaluate dresses, and what their policies are for returning unsold dresses. You should also ask if they require an appointment to evaluate your dress or if you can walk in during business hours.

Evaluating Your Dress

When you bring your dress to the consignment shop, they will evaluate it to determine its condition and value. They will look for any damage, stains, or alterations that may affect the dress's resale value. They will also consider the designer, style, and age of the dress.

Based on their evaluation, the shop will determine a fair price to sell your dress for. This price will be based on what they believe the dress is worth in their market and what they think it will sell for. It is important to remember that the price they set is negotiable, so don't be afraid to ask for a higher price if you feel your dress is worth more.

Preparing Your Dress for Sale

Before bringing your dress to the consignment shop, it is important to prepare it for sale. This means having it professionally cleaned and pressed to ensure it is in the best possible condition. You should also remove any personal items, such as wedding jewelry or undergarments, and provide the shop with any original tags or receipts you may have.

When you bring your dress to the shop, make sure to bring it in a garment bag to protect it during transport. You should also be prepared to sign a contract with the shop that outlines their commission rates, policies, and the price they will sell your dress for.

Question and Answer

Q: What percentage of the sale price does the consignment shop take?

A: The commission rates vary by shop but are typically between 30-50% of the sale price.

Q: How long will it take for my dress to sell?

A: The time it takes for your dress to sell depends on a variety of factors, including the demand for your dress's style and the current market conditions. It is important to be patient and not get discouraged if your dress doesn't sell right away.

Q: What happens if my dress doesn't sell?

A: Most consignment shops have policies in place for unsold dresses. They may offer to return the dress to you or continue to try to sell it at a discounted price.

Q: Can I set the price for my dress?

A: While the consignment shop will ultimately determine the price of your dress, you can discuss your desired price with them during the evaluation process. It is important to be realistic about the value of your dress and to trust the shop's expertise in setting a fair price.

Conclusion of Consignment Shops Buy Wedding Dresses

Selling your wedding dress at a consignment shop can be a great way to free up space in your closet and make some money in the process. By following the steps outlined in this post, you can ensure that you find the right consignment shop and get a fair price for your dress. Remember to be patient and trust the shop's expertise in setting a price that will sell your dress while also being fair to you.
