The Meaning Of Wedding Dresses Understanding The Symbolism Behind The Gown

Written by Frank Aug 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Wedding dresses are one of the most recognizable symbols of a wedding. From the traditional white gown to the modern, colorful dresses, they carry a lot of meaning and symbolism. But what do they really mean? Why do brides wear them? And why are they so important?

Choosing a wedding dress can be a daunting task for any bride-to-be. It's not just about finding the right style or fit, but also about understanding the significance of the gown itself. Wedding dresses represent a bride's personality, style, and beliefs. They are also a reflection of the cultural and historical traditions that have shaped weddings throughout the ages.

At its core, the meaning of a wedding dress is a symbol of purity, virginity, and innocence. It represents the bride's commitment to her future husband and the sanctity of their union. It is also a sign of her willingness to start a new life and embrace the responsibilities that come with it.

In addition to its symbolic meaning, wedding dresses have also evolved over time to reflect changing fashion trends and cultural norms. While white has traditionally been the color of choice for wedding dresses in Western cultures, other cultures and religions have their own customs and traditions when it comes to wedding attire. For example, in India, brides often wear red or other bright colors to symbolize good luck and prosperity.

The History of Wedding Dresses: A Personal Experience

My grandmother's wedding dress is a perfect example of how wedding dresses have evolved over time. Her dress, which she wore in the 1950s, was a simple A-line gown made of satin and lace. It had a high neckline and long sleeves, reflecting the modesty and conservatism of the era. The dress was also a reflection of my grandmother's personality – simple, elegant, and understated.

Today, wedding dresses are available in a wide range of styles, colors, and fabrics. From traditional ball gowns to sleek, modern designs, brides can choose a dress that reflects their personal style and taste. Some brides even opt for non-traditional wedding attire, such as pantsuits or jumpsuits, to make a statement and break with tradition.

The Symbolism of White Wedding Dresses

While wedding dresses come in all colors and styles, white remains the most popular choice for Western brides. The tradition of wearing a white wedding dress dates back to the Victorian era, when Queen Victoria wore a white gown to her wedding in 1840. At the time, white was not a common color for wedding dresses, but Victoria's choice set a new trend that has endured to this day.

White wedding dresses are often associated with purity, innocence, and virginity. They are also seen as a symbol of the bride's commitment to her future husband and their new life together. However, not all cultures share this tradition – for example, in many Asian cultures, red is the traditional color for wedding attire, symbolizing good luck and happiness.

The Importance of Choosing the Right Wedding Dress

Choosing the right wedding dress is an important decision for any bride-to-be. Not only does it reflect her personal style and taste, but it also carries a lot of symbolism and meaning. When choosing a wedding dress, it's important to consider the cultural and historical traditions that have shaped weddings throughout the ages, as well as your own personal beliefs and values.

Tips for Choosing the Perfect Wedding Dress

1. Start early – it can take months to find the perfect dress, so give yourself plenty of time to shop around and try on different styles.

2. Consider your venue and wedding theme – your dress should complement the overall look and feel of your wedding.

3. Think about your body type – choose a dress that flatters your figure and makes you feel confident and beautiful.

4. Don't be afraid to break with tradition – if a traditional white gown isn't your style, consider other options like colored dresses or non-traditional wedding attire.

FAQs: Answering Your Questions About Wedding Dresses

Q: What is the meaning of the veil?

A: The veil is a traditional part of the bride's attire and symbolizes modesty, purity, and innocence.

Q: Why do brides wear "something old, something new, something borrowed, and something blue"?

A: This tradition dates back to Victorian times and is meant to bring good luck to the bride. "Something old" represents the bride's past, "something new" represents her future, "something borrowed" represents borrowed happiness, and "something blue" represents fidelity and love.

Q: Can I wear a colored wedding dress?

A: Yes! While white is the traditional color for wedding dresses in Western cultures, brides are free to choose any color or style that reflects their personal taste and style.

Q: Can I wear a pantsuit or jumpsuit instead of a dress?

A: Absolutely! Non-traditional wedding attire is becoming increasingly popular, and many brides choose to wear pantsuits or jumpsuits instead of dresses.

Conclusion: Understanding the Meaning of Wedding Dresses

Wedding dresses are more than just a piece of clothing – they are a symbol of love, commitment, and tradition. Whether you choose a traditional white gown or a non-traditional wedding outfit, your dress should reflect your personal style and values. By understanding the meaning and symbolism behind wedding dresses, you can choose a dress that is not only beautiful, but also meaningful and significant.
