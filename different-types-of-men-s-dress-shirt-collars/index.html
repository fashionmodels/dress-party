Different Types Of Men s Dress Shirt Collars A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Oct 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of wearing the same old dress shirt with a plain, boring collar? Do you want to know more about the different types of men's dress shirt collars and how to choose the right one for your look and occasion? Look no further, this guide has got you covered!

Choosing the right collar for your dress shirt can be a daunting task. Different types of collars can complement different face shapes, tie knots, and dress codes. The wrong collar choice can make you look out of place, uncomfortable, or even unprofessional.

So, what are the different types of men's dress shirt collars, and what are their targets?

In this guide, we will cover the most popular types of men's dress shirt collars and their targets. We will discuss the history, characteristics, and styling tips for each collar type, along with related keywords to help you search and learn more.

The Classic Point Collar

When most people think of a dress shirt collar, they picture a classic point collar. This collar is the most common and versatile type of men's dress shirt collar. Its points are angled downward, and the collar spread can vary from narrow to wide, depending on the shirt's style and the wearer's preference.

I remember wearing a point collar shirt to my first job interview. I paired it with a small knot tie and a navy suit. The collar helped frame my face and tie, giving me a sharp and confident look. I learned that the classic point collar is suitable for most face shapes and tie knots, making it a safe and timeless choice for any occasion.

The Button-Down Collar

The button-down collar is a popular choice for casual and preppy looks. Its collar points are fastened to the shirt with buttons, which prevent them from flapping or shifting around. This collar type originated from polo players who wanted to keep their collars from flying up during the match.

I like wearing a button-down collar shirt with a knit tie and a blazer. The collar's buttoned-down look adds a relaxed and playful touch to the outfit, while the tie and blazer keep it polished and sophisticated. The button-down collar is suitable for round and square face shapes, and it pairs well with small to medium knot ties.

The Spread Collar

The spread collar is a modern and fashion-forward collar type. Its collar points are angled outward and away from the face, creating a wider and more open collar spread. The spread collar is a popular choice for formal dress codes, as it allows for a larger tie knot and a more prominent collar presence.

I wore a spread collar shirt to a black-tie event once. I paired it with a Windsor knot tie and a tuxedo. The spread collar's wide and sharp look complemented the bold and elegant tie knot, making me stand out from the crowd. The spread collar is suitable for oval and long face shapes, and it pairs well with larger knot ties.

The Club Collar

The club collar is a unique and vintage collar type. Its collar points are rounded instead of pointed, giving it a softer and more playful look. The club collar originated from English private school uniforms, where the rounded collar points symbolized membership to a club or society.

I have a club collar shirt that I like to wear with a bow tie and suspenders. The collar's rounded shape adds a whimsical and charming touch to the outfit, while the bow tie and suspenders keep it classy and dapper. The club collar is suitable for angular and sharp face shapes, and it pairs well with bow ties and accessories.

Conclusion of Different Types of Men's Dress Shirt Collars

Choosing the right collar for your dress shirt can enhance your style and confidence. We hope this guide has helped you understand the different types of men's dress shirt collars and their targets. Remember, the classic point collar is a safe and timeless choice, the button-down collar is a casual and playful choice, the spread collar is a formal and fashion-forward choice, and the club collar is a vintage and charming choice. Experiment with different collar types and see which one suits you best. Happy dressing!
